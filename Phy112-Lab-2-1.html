In this lab, you will explore capacitors and see how they are used in circuits. The capacitor is an extremely important electrical circuit device and are present on almost every real circuit. For direct current circuits, the capacitor creates a large electric field between two conducting surfaces, which serve as a reserve of extra electrons. The capacitor is said to be charging when the E field is increasing, and discharging when the E field is decreasing. For AC circuits these devices also serve as a filter for high or low frequency signals.
