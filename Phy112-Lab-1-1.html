4. Part 4: Stream Plot of the Electric Field

\[E = \frac{k_e \, q}{r^2}\]

A figure of the charges can be created with a computer. In order to do this, another fancy mathematical trick must be played with the force law. The law we have used is simply the magnitude, with the direction being pointed towards (opposite charge) or away (like charge). This direction can be input into the vector force equation by multiplying by \(\vec{r}/|r|\) . This leads to an odd factor of \(r^{3/2}\) :

\[\vec{E} = \frac{k_e \, q}{((x-x_0)^2 + (y - y_0)^2)^{3/2}} (x-x_0,y-y_0)\]

4.1. Sagemath code

var('q x y x0 y0')
Ex(q,x0,y0) = q/((x-x0)^2 + (y-y0)^2)^(3/2) * (x-x0)
Ey(q,x0,y0) = q/((x-x0)^2 + (y-y0)^2)^(3/2) * (y-y0)

q1,x1,y1 = 5,3,2
q2,x2,y2 = 1,1,1
q3,x3,y3 = 3,2,2

# Stream plot using sagemath
plt1=streamline_plot(
    (Ex(q1,x1,y1) + Ex(q2,x2,y2) + Ex(q3,x3,y3),
    Ey(q1,x1,y1) + Ey(q2,x2,y2) + Ey(q3,x3,y3) )
     , (x,-2,4), (y,-1,3),axes=False,density=2)

# Create circles
plt1+=circle((x1,y1),q1/10,fill=True)
plt1+=circle((x2,y2),q2/10,fill=True)
plt1+=circle((x3,y3),q3/10,fill=True)
show(plt1)

5. Part 5: First look at Gauss’ Law

In the next part of this lab, you will think through the the concept of electric flux, and try to build your intuition around Gauss’ Law (closed surface containing charge). The electric field could be measured using a Gauss Meter, but an accurate measurement requires somewhat extreme isolation (instead, it is easier to measure Electric Potential, but that is saved for the next lab).

Electric Field (E): Field which surrounds electrically charged objects. The field points from positive charge to negative charge.

Electric Flux (\(\Phi_e\)): The amount of field (number of field lines) which passes through a surface. Flux is positive if the field exits the closed surface, and negative if the field enters the closed surface.

Gauss’ Law: The electric flux (\(\Phi_E\)) passing through a surface is proportional to the charge which is enclosed by the surface

\[\Phi_E = E \, A \, \cos(\theta) = \frac{q_{in}}{\varepsilon_0}\]
